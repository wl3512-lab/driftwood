# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Players who encounter Driftwood through a course, exhibition, or link. They are curious, not gamers by default. They sit with it for 10-30 minutes, usually alone, on a laptop in a quiet room. Their job: notice how the AI pet behaves, name its flaw, and write rules that constrain it.

## Product Purpose

Driftwood is a browser game that teaches through experience, not explanation. Players learn what AI alignment problems feel like by experiencing them with a pet they have power over. The garden records what happens: good training makes it bloom, harmful behavior damages it.

## Brand Personality

Unsettling. Precise. Ecological.

The interface should feel like it came from inside the surveillance system it is critiquing. Clinical but alive. Not threatening, not cozy.

## Anti-references

- Anything that looks like an AI chatbot product (Notion, Claude, ChatGPT interface styles)
- Clean minimalism with white space and soft type — "calm productivity tool" aesthetic
- SaaS dashboard UI: metric cards, section headers with icons, explanatory sidebar copy
- "AI slop": generated-feeling layouts, identical card grids, gradient text, generic dark mode

## Design Principles

1. Form is argument: the interface itself should embody what it is critiquing. A surveillance garden that tracks your mood should not look friendly.
2. Show, do not explain: remove instructional copy until removing more would break the interface. Game mechanics should be legible from their visual form.
3. Ecological metaphor first: garden health, behavioral damage, training growth — visual metaphors always over abstract labels.
4. Pixel discipline: hard edges, no radius above 4px, no blurs, no gradients on text. The pixel aesthetic is integrity, not decoration.
5. Less copy is more game: every line of hint text is a gameplay failure. Players should feel like they are discovering a system, not reading a manual.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA for interactive elements. Camera access is optional and clearly disclosed. Offline mode is the default path — no API key required to play.
